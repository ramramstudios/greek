Reference Lexica

LSJ — Liddell, Scott, Jones

A Greek-English Lexicon, 9th ed. 1940; Supplement 1996. Headword: χριστός
IOf things: "to be rubbed on, used as ointment or salve" — opp. πιστός (Aesch. Pr. 480; Eur. Hipp. 516)
IIaOf persons: anointed priests — LXX Lev 4:5, 4:16, 6:22
IIbKings of Israel: χριστὸς κυρίου — LXX 1 Sam 24:7; Ps 17:51
IIcPlural: χριστοί — LXX Hab 3:13
IIINT: ὁ χ. = the Messiah (Heb. מָשִׁיחַ) — as title Matt 2:4; as proper name Matt 1:1

LSJ gives minimal treatment to NT and patristic usage. Thayer's NT lexicon supplements those stages. Full LSJ entry reconstruction →

Thayer — A Greek-English Lexicon of the New Testament

J.H. Thayer, 1889. Headword: Χριστός
1ὁ Χριστός as Jewish messianic title (the expected deliverer)
2Applied to Jesus as fulfillment of OT messianic role
3Title becoming proper name: Ἰησοῦς Χριστός
4ψευδόχριστοι (false Christs) — confirms titular technical status

BDB — Brown, Driver, Briggs

A Hebrew and English Lexicon of the Old Testament, 1906. Headword: מָשַׁח / מָשִׁיחַ

māšaḥ = to anoint/smear (verb); māšîaḥ = anointed one (qal passive ptcp. substantivized). Primary referents: priests (Lev), kings (Sam/Ps), Cyrus (Isa 45:1), future leader (Dan 9:25–26).

Hebrew root מָשַׁח is structurally parallel to Greek χρίω; both yield verbal adjectives meaning "anointed one."

Stage 1 — Classical Greek: Pharmaceutical Adjective

Stage 1 · Classical

c. 460–1st century BCE Pharmaceutical context Religious content: none

Semantic function: Literal adjective: "smearable, applicable (as ointment/salve)"
Paradigmatic opposition: χριστά ≠ πιστά / ποτά (topical vs. drinkable medicines)
Morphological status: Verbal adjective (from χρίω); modifies things, not persons

Aeschylus · Prometheus Vinctus · Line 480 · TLG 0085.003
φάρμακα … χριστά τε καὶ πιστά
"medicines … smearable and drinkable"
pharmaceutical
⚠ AUTHORSHIP DISPUTED: M.L. West (JHS 99, 1979) and M. Griffith (1983) argue post-Aeschylean authorship; attribution to Aeschylus is traditional but contested. Source: LSJ s.v. χριστός I; Perseus:text:1999.01.0010
Euripides · Hippolytus · Line 516 · TLG 0006.005 · 428 BCE
πότερα δὲ χριστὸν ἢ ποτὸν τὸ φάρμακον;
"Is the drug a smearable [salve] or a drinkable [potion]?"
pharmaceutical
✓ Securely Euripidean — 428 BCE performance date recorded by ancient vita. Source: LSJ s.v. χριστός I; Perseus:text:1999.01.0105
Hippocratic Corpus · De ulceribus and passim · TLG 0627.007 · c. 400 BCE
χριστά φάρμακα / χριστέον (from χρίω)
Topically applied medicines / "must be rubbed on"
pharmaceutical
⚠ MORPHOLOGICAL CAUTION: De ulceribus 18 uses χρηστέον — a gerundive from χράομαι ("to use"), NOT from χρίω ("to anoint"). These are distinct roots. Genuine χριστά forms (from χρίω) appear elsewhere in the Hippocratic corpus but require full TLG credential access to confirm specific passages.

Stage 2a — LXX: Priestly Anointing Title

Stage 2a · Hellenistic

LXX Pentateuch translation c. 250–200 BCE Religious: Priestly / ritual

Semantic function: Institutional title: "the anointed priest" — renders H. הַכֹּהֵן הַמָּשִׁיחַ
Translation mechanism: Structural calque — χριστός chosen as morphological equivalent of Hebrew מָשִׁיחַ (both = verbal adj. of "anoint")
Morphological status: Attributive adjective in frozen title: ὁ ἱερεὺς ὁ χριστός

LXX Leviticus 4:5 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
ὁ ἱερεὺς ὁ χριστὸς ὁ τετελειωμένος τὰς χεῖρας
"the anointed priest who has been ordained"
Hebrew: הַכֹּהֵן הַמָּשִׁיחַ
priestly_ritual_title
LXX Leviticus 4:16 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
ὁ ἱερεὺς ὁ χριστός
"the anointed priest"
Hebrew: הַכֹּהֵן הַמָּשִׁיחַ
priestly_ritual_title
LXX Leviticus 6:22 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
ὁ ἱερεὺς ὁ χριστὸς ἀντ᾿ αὐτοῦ
"the anointed priest who succeeds him"
Hebrew: הַכֹּהֵן הַמָּשִׁיחַ
priestly_ritual_title
LXX Leviticus 21:10 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
τὸ ἔλαιον τὸ χριστόν
"the anointing oil"
ritual_oil_application
Bridges Stage 1 and Stage 2a: χριστός still modifies an object (oil) but in a religious ritual context.
LXX Leviticus 4:3 · Rahlfs-Hanhart · Perfect passive participle
ὁ ἀρχιερεὺς ὁ κεχρισμένος
"the anointed high priest"
priestly_ritual_title
κεχρισμένος (perfect passive ptcp., NOT the adjective χριστός) — both render the same Hebrew הַמָּשִׁיחַ; LXX translators alternate between the two forms without semantic distinction.

Stage 2b — LXX: Royal Davidic Title

Stage 2b · Hellenistic

Historical books and Psalms, c. 200–150 BCE Religious: Royal / Davidic covenant

Semantic function: Royal title: "the LORD's anointed" — renders H. מְשִׁיחַ יְהוָה / מְשִׁיחִי
Morphological status: Near-formulaic construction: χριστὸς κυρίου (nom.) / χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ (gen.) / τῷ χριστῷ (dat.)

LXX 1 Samuel (A' Basileion) 2:10 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
ὑψώσει κέρας χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ
"will exalt the horn of his anointed"
royal_prospective
Hannah's prayer — reference is PROSPECTIVE (no king yet at this narrative point); one of the earliest LXX passages with any forward-looking force for χριστός.
LXX 1 Samuel 24:7 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
τῷ χριστῷ κυρίου … χριστὸς κυρίου ἐστὶν οὗτος
"to the LORD's anointed … because this is the LORD's anointed"
royal_current
PARADIGMATIC royal use: Saul as current χριστὸς κυρίου. The formula χριστὸς κυρίου ἐστίν is the clearest predicative title construction.
LXX Psalm 2:2 (= MT 2:2) · Rahlfs-Hanhart
κατὰ τοῦ κυρίου καὶ κατὰ τοῦ χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ
"against the LORD and against his anointed"
royal_proto_messianic
Most-cited LXX messianic verse in the NT. Acts 4:25–26 applies this verse to Jesus, Herod, and Pilate. Its "royal" reference becomes proto-messianic through NT re-reading.
LXX Isaiah 45:1 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
τῷ χριστῷ μου Κύρῳ
"to my anointed — Cyrus"
non_israelite_royal
UNIQUE: the only LXX application of χριστός to a non-Israelite. Decouples the title from Davidic lineage — proves χριστός = functionally "divinely authorized agent," not "ethnic insider."
LXX Habakkuk 3:13 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
τοὺς χριστούς σου
"your anointed ones"
royal_collective_plural
Masc. acc. PLURAL — the only plural instance in LSJ's LXX citations. The NT NEVER uses plural χριστοί in this collective sense; the NT singularizes the title exclusively onto Jesus.
LXX Lamentations 4:20 · Rahlfs-Hanhart
χριστὸς κυρίου συνελήμφθη ἐν ταῖς διαφθοραῖς αὐτῶν
"the LORD's anointed was caught in their traps"
royal_elegiac
Historical referent: King Zedekiah, captured 586 BCE.

Stage 2c — LXX: Proto-Messianic (Daniel)

Stage 2c · Hellenistic

Daniel translation c. 100 BCE; Theodotion revision c. 2nd century CE Proto-eschatological

Semantic function: Future-pointing anointed figure in a numerical ("70 weeks") schema

Daniel 9:25 (Theodotion text) · Rahlfs-Hanhart
ἕως χριστοῦ ἡγουμένου ἑβδομάδες ἑπτά
"until an anointed leader, seven weeks"
proto_messianic_eschatological
ANARTHROUS — no article signals proto-name status. Future referent within the "70 weeks" schema. Foundational for Second Temple messianic chronology.
Daniel 9:26 · LXX vs. Theodotion — DISPUTED TEXT
LXX: ἐξολοθρευθήσεται χρῖσμα — "an anointing will be cut off"
Theodotion: ἐξολοθρευθήσεται χριστός — "an anointed one will be cut off"
proto_messianic_disputed_text
LXX and Theodotion diverge on whether the referent is the abstract noun χρῖσμα or the title χριστός. Theodotion's version becomes standard in Christian citation. The variant itself reveals LXX translators navigating the title/abstract-noun boundary.

Stage 3 — Second Temple: Explicitly Eschatological Messianic Title

Stage 3 · Hellenistic/Roman Transition

Psalms of Solomon c. 50 BCE Future Davidic deliverer

Semantic function: Title for a FUTURE Davidic deliverer — not a current reigning king
Note: This evidence is not found in the primary project TLG files — it is critical supplementary evidence added in the diachronic analysis.

Psalms of Solomon 17:32 · Ed. Rahlfs; R.B. Wright in OTP vol. 2 (Charlesworth, 1985) · c. 50 BCE
βασιλεὺς αὐτῶν χριστὸς κυρίου
"their king, the Lord's anointed"
eschatological_future_messianic
CRUCIAL: the king is FUTURE (no Davidic king reigns; Hasmoneans considered illegitimate by the Psalms of Solomon community). χριστὸς κυρίου without article — title is shedding the article = moving toward name-status. This is genuine eschatological messianism pre-dating the NT. Significance: Strongest pre-NT evidence for χριστός as a genuinely future eschatological title.
Psalms of Solomon 18:7 · Ed. Rahlfs · c. 50 BCE
ὑπὸ ῥάβδον παιδείας χριστοῦ κυρίου
"under the rod of discipline of the Lord's anointed"
eschatological_future_messianic

Stage 4a — New Testament: Paul (Earliest Layer)

Stage 4a · Roman

c. 49–62 CE Proper name / title in transition ἐν Χριστῷ formula
1 Thessalonians 1:1 · TLG 0031.012 · c. 50 CE · NA28
ἐν θεῷ πατρὶ καὶ κυρίῳ Ἰησοῦ Χριστῷ
"in God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ"
proper_name_formula
Earliest datable Christian document. Compound name Ἰησοῦς Χριστός fully established by c. 50 CE.
1 Corinthians 15:3 · TLG 0031.006 · c. 54 CE · NA28
Χριστὸς ἀπέθανεν ὑπὲρ τῶν ἁμαρτιῶν ἡμῶν κατὰ τὰς γραφάς
"Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures"
proper_name_creedal
Received pre-Pauline tradition (παρέδωκα … ὃ παρέλαβον). Anarthrous Χριστός as sentence subject = proper name fully onomastic. Underlying tradition inferred to date c. 35–40 CE (Dunn, Theology of Paul, pp. 235–238).
1 Corinthians 1:23 · TLG 0031.006 · c. 54 CE · NA28
Χριστὸν ἐσταυρωμένον, Ἰουδαίοις μὲν σκάνδαλον
"Christ crucified — a stumbling block to Jews"
proper_name_resemanticized
Paul explicitly acknowledges that "Christ crucified" is a RESEMANTICIZATION of the messianic title: crucifixion ≠ expected messianic victory (σκάνδαλον = scandal). The title has been radically redefined.
Galatians 3:28 · TLG 0031.009 · c. 55 CE · NA28
εἷς ἐστε ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ
"you are all one in Christ Jesus"
proper_name_locative
ἐν Χριστῷ formula appears ~164 times in the Pauline corpus. Using locative dative with a person's name is a purely onomastic construction — the word is fully a proper name.

Stage 4b — New Testament: Synoptic Gospels

Stage 4b · Roman

c. 70–90 CE Both title (ὁ Χριστός) and proper name (Ἰησοῦς Χριστός)
Matthew 1:1 · TLG 0031.001 · NA28
Βίβλος γενέσεως Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ, υἱοῦ Δαυίδ
"The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, son of David"
proper_name
Incipit: Χριστός without article = proper name (cognomen) in opening formula.
Matthew 16:16 · TLG 0031.001 · NA28
Σὺ εἶ ὁ Χριστὸς ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ θεοῦ τοῦ ζῶντος
"You are the Christ, the Son of the living God"
messianic_title
Peter's confession: ὁ Χριστός WITH article = live messianic title in narrative context. Same author, same text, different article usage = title vs. name distinction still operative.
Matthew 24:24 · TLG 0031.001 · NA28
ψευδόχριστοι καὶ ψευδοπροφῆται
"false Christs and false prophets"
title_formation_confirmed
COMPOUND ψευδόχριστοι = "false Christs." The existence of a category of false claimants proves χριστός is sufficiently technical that impostors are conceivable — title formation is complete.

Stage 4c — New Testament: Johannine Literature

Stage 4c · Roman

c. 90–100 CE Bilingual gloss; stated Gospel purpose
John 1:41 · TLG 0031.004 · NA28 · KEY PASSAGE
Εὑρήκαμεν τὸν Μεσσίαν, ὅ ἐστιν μεθερμηνευόμενον ὁ Χριστός
"We have found the Messiah, which is translated 'the Christ'"
bilingual_translation_gloss
THE KEY PASSAGE: the only NT occurrence of an explicit bilingual translation gloss. Establishes: Μεσσίας (Aramaic loanword) = ὁ Χριστός (Greek calque). The formula μεθερμηνευόμενον ("which is translated") is the metalinguistic marker proving the author knew these were two words for the same concept.
John 4:25 · TLG 0031.004 · NA28
Μεσσίας ἔρχεται, ὁ λεγόμενος Χριστός
"The Messiah is coming, the one called Christ"
bilingual_translation_gloss
Second bilingual gloss in John. ὁ λεγόμενος Χριστός = the same distancing/explanatory formula used by Josephus (Ant. 20.200). Here it is explanatory for Greek readers.
John 20:31 · TLG 0031.004 · NA28
ἵνα πιστεύ[σ]ητε ὅτι Ἰησοῦς ἐστιν ὁ Χριστὸς ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ θεοῦ
"that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God"
christological_thesis
The Gospel's stated purpose: the identification "Jesus = the Christ" is the organizing theological claim. ὁ Χριστός with double article = maximum definiteness.

Stage 4d — External Attestation: Josephus

Stage 4d · Roman (Non-Christian Source)

c. 93–94 CE TLG 0526.001 (Antiquitates Judaicae)
Josephus · Antiquitates Judaicae 20.200 · Niese ed. · c. 93–94 CE
τὸν ἀδελφὸν Ἰησοῦ τοῦ λεγομένου Χριστοῦ, Ἰάκωβος ὄνομα αὐτῷ
"the brother of Jesus who was called Christ, James by name"
proper_name_distanced_external
✓ AUTHENTIC — widely accepted by scholars; no Christian interpolation suspected.
τοῦ λεγομένου Χριστοῦ: Josephus assumes his readers KNOW what "Christ" designates and uses λεγόμενος ("so-called") to distance himself from endorsement — not to explain an obscure term. Confirms name-status by 93 CE for a Jewish author writing for a Roman audience.
Josephus · Antiquitates Judaicae 18.63–64 (Testimonium Flavianum) · Niese ed.
ὁ χριστὸς οὗτος ἦν
"he was the Christ"
disputed_external_reference
⚠ DISPUTED AUTHENTICITY: Current form reflects probable Christian embellishment; no surviving "neutral" Greek manuscript. Modern reconstructions (Meier, Marginal Jew vol. 1, pp. 56–88) are scholarly hypotheses. The passage cannot be used as secure Josephan evidence.

Stage 5 — Early Church: Fixed Proper Name

Stage 5 · Roman (Early Patristic)

c. 96–165 CE Generates new vocabulary (Χριστιανισμός) Requires etymological explanation for outsiders

Note: This stage evidence was not in the original project TLG files — it was added in the diachronic analysis phase.

1 Clement 1:1 · TLG 1271.001 · c. 96 CE
…κλητοῖς ἡγιασμένοις ἐν θελήματι θεοῦ διὰ τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ
"to the called, sanctified by the will of God through our Lord Jesus Christ"
proper_name_liturgical
Χριστός is purely formulaic in 1 Clement's salutation — no messianic explanation is offered or needed; it IS the proper name, functioning like a cognomen.
Ignatius of Antioch · Epistle to the Philadelphians · TLG 1443.001 · c. 110 CE · Ed. Schoedel, Hermeneia (1985)
Χριστιανισμός (Phld. 6:1)
"Christianity" — abstract noun derived from the proper name
proper_name_with_neologism
Key forms in Ignatius: κυρίου Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ (salutation — proper name compound); ὅσοι γὰρ θεοῦ εἰσιν καὶ Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ (3:2 — partitive gen.); Χριστιανισμός (6:1 — NEOLOGISM); ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ τῇ κοινῇ ἐλπίδι ἡμῶν (11:2 — locative).

SEALS Stage 5: The -ισμός abstract suffix is ONLY attached to proper names, never to titles. Χριστιανισμός proves beyond doubt that Χριστός is functioning as a proper name by c. 110 CE.
Justin Martyr · First Apology Ch. 4 · TLG 0645.001 · c. 155 CE · Ed. Marcovich (De Gruyter, 1994)
Justin defends the NAME "Χριστιανοί" against Roman charges
proper_name_opaque_requiring_defence
Justin plays on confusion: Χριστιανοί (from Χριστός = "anointed") vs. χρηστοί / Χρηστιανοί (from χρηστός = "good"). The fact that Justin must ARGUE the name is not blameworthy, and must distinguish it from a similarly-sounding Greek adjective, proves Χριστός is an OPAQUE PROPER NAME to non-Christian Greek speakers by c. 155 CE.
Justin Martyr · Dialogue with Trypho Ch. 86 · TLG 0645.003 · c. 158 CE · Ed. Marcovich (De Gruyter, 1997)
Justin explains WHY Jesus is called χριστός by etymological recovery
proper_name_etymologically_recovered
Justin explains that Jesus is called χριστός because he was "anointed" (κεχρισμένος) as king, priest, and prophet. This ETYMOLOGICAL RECOVERY — explaining to an audience WHY a word means what it means — is the definitive sign that χριστός has become an opaque proper name whose original meaning is no longer transparent.

Analytical Summary

Question 1 — The Borrowing Model

VERDICT: "Borrowing into Hebrew and back into Greek" is an inaccurate description.

Correct model — SEMANTIC CALQUE: Greek χριστός was chosen as a structural translation equivalent for Hebrew מָשִׁיחַ; Hebrew never borrowed the Greek word. The direction of semantic transfer is Hebrew → Greek through LXX translation. Μεσσίας (John 1:41) is a separate LOANWORD channel (Aramaic → Greek), not a reversal of the calque.

Semantic Stages Summary

  1. Stage 1: Pure pharmaceutical adjective (Classical) — LITERAL
  2. Stage 2a: Priestly title via LXX calque — LEXICAL TRANSLATION
  3. Stage 2b: Royal formulaic title — TITLE FORMATION
  4. Stage 2c: Proto-messianic (Daniel) — PROTO-ESCHATOLOGICAL
  5. Stage 3: Explicit future messianic (PsSol) — ESCHATOLOGICAL TITLE
  6. Stage 4 (a–d): NT — name/title in transition, resemanticized — RESEMANTICIZATION
  7. Stage 5: Early church — fixed proper name — ONOMASTIC

Strongest Evidence for the Title → Name Transition

  • Anarthrous Χριστός as sentence subject: 1 Cor 15:3
  • Derived appellative Χριστιανοί: Acts 11:26
  • Neologism Χριστιανισμός: Ignatius Phld. 6:1
  • Need to explain etymology to outsiders: Justin 1 Apol. 4; Dial. 86
