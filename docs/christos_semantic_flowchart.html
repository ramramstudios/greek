Methodological Note

The diagram traces a semantic calque process, not a borrowing. Greek χριστός (verbal adj. from χρίω, "to anoint") was chosen by the LXX translators as a structural equivalent for Hebrew מָשִׁיחַ (māšîaḥ). Hebrew semantic content migrated into the Greek word through translation. The parallel Μεσσίας channel is a separate loanword from Aramaic מְשִׁיחָא.

Stage Legend

Stage 1 — Classical

c. 460–400 BCE. Pure pharmaceutical adjective: "smearable" (χριστά φάρμακα). No religious content. Aeschylus, Euripides, Hippocrates.

Stage 2a — LXX Priestly

c. 250 BCE. LXX calque of Hebrew הַכֹּהֵן הַמָּשִׁיחַ: "the anointed priest." Institutional title in frozen construction.

Stage 2b–2c — LXX Royal / Proto-Messianic

c. 200–100 BCE. Royal formula χριστὸς κυρίου in Samuel, Psalms. Proto-eschatological in Daniel 9:25 (Theodotion).

Stage 3 — Psalms of Solomon ★

c. 50 BCE. First genuinely eschatological use: χριστὸς κυρίου as a FUTURE deliverer (PsSol 17:32). Critical bridge to NT.

Loanword Channel — Μεσσίας

Aramaic מְשִׁיחָא transliterated into Greek. Separate channel: Μεσσίας = borrowed form; ὁ Χριστός = calque. Converge at John 1:41.

Stage 4 — NT (Paul → Gospels → John)

c. 50–100 CE. Title/name in transition. Anarthrous Χριστός = proper name (Paul). ὁ Χριστός = messianic title (Gospels). John 1:41 explicit bilingual gloss.

Stage 5 — Early Church

c. 96–160 CE. Fixed opaque proper name. Χριστιανισμός (Ignatius) seals onomastic status. Justin Martyr must explain the etymology.

Methodological Notes

Semantic Calque vs. Borrowing

The correct model for the Hebrew-Greek transfer is semantic calque, not borrowing. Hebrew מָשִׁיחַ was never borrowed into Greek; Greek χριστός was never borrowed into Hebrew. The LXX translators chose χριστός as the Greek structural equivalent of מָשִׁיחַ on the basis of parallel morphology (both = verbal adjective of "to anoint"). Hebrew semantic content migrated into the Greek word through repeated use as its translation equivalent across ~38–40 LXX passages.

★ Psalms of Solomon

PsSol 17:32 and 18:7 are not in the LXX canon but are the most important pre-NT evidence for χριστός as a genuinely eschatological title (a FUTURE deliverer, not a current king). These texts are entirely absent from the original project files and were added in the diachronic analysis. Their absence is the most significant gap in the initial research.

Onomastic Crystallization

The transition from title to proper name is confirmed by four independent criteria: (1) anarthrous Χριστός as sentence subject (1 Cor 15:3); (2) derived appellative Χριστιανοί (Acts 11:26, Josephus Ant. 18.64); (3) abstract neologism Χριστιανισμός (Ignatius Phld. 6:1); (4) need for etymological explanation by Justin Martyr (1 Apol. 4) — the surest sign a word has become opaque.
