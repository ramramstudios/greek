χριστός, -ή, -όν    verbal adjective of χρίω

Literal etymology:
  χρίω  ("to anoint, rub/smear with oil or fat")
     + -τός  (verbal adjective suffix indicating "capable of / subject to"
               the action of the verb)
  → χριστός: "that which is/has been/is to be anointed or smeared on"

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I.  OF THINGS:  "to be rubbed on, used as ointment or salve"
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Opposed to πιστός / ποτός ("drinkable"):

  ● Aeschylus, Prometheus Vinctus 480
    χριστά φάρμακα — "ointments / smearable medicines"
    [Prometheus enumerating gifts to humankind: knowledge of topical vs.
     oral medicaments]

  ● Euripides, Hippolytus 516
    χριστὸν ἢ ποτὸν τὸ φάρμακον — "is the drug a salve or a potion?"
    [Phaedra's question to the Nurse about the love-charm]

  Pharmacological contrast: χριστά (topical applications) vs. πιστά (oral
  medicines) is the standard Classical Greek distinction in medical writing.
  The Hippocratic corpus uses this contrast throughout surgical and
  pharmaceutical treatises.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II.  OF PERSONS:  "anointed"
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  IIa. ANOINTED PRIESTS (LXX — Leviticus):
       ὁ ἱερεὺς ὁ χριστός = "the anointed priest" (= H. הַכֹּהֵן הַמָּשִׁיחַ)
       LXX Leviticus 4:3, 4:5, 4:16, 6:22

       τὸ ἔλαιον τὸ χριστόν = "the anointing oil"
       LXX Leviticus 21:10

  IIb. ANOINTED KINGS (LXX — Samuel, Psalms, Lamentations, Habakkuk):
       ὁ χριστὸς κυρίου = "the LORD's anointed" (= H. מְשִׁיחַ יְהוָה)

       LXX 1 Samuel 2:10  —  ὑψώσει κέρας χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ
       LXX 1 Samuel 24:7  —  τῷ χριστῷ κυρίου / χριστὸς κυρίου ἐστὶν
       LXX Psalm 2:2       —  κατὰ τοῦ χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ
       LXX Psalm 17:51 [MT 18:50]  —  τῷ χριστῷ αὐτοῦ τῷ Δαυίδ
       LXX Lamentations 4:20  —  χριστὸς κυρίου συνελήμφθη

  IIc. PLURAL USAGE:
       χριστοί σου = "your anointed ones" (collective)
       LXX Habakkuk 3:13  — τοὺς χριστούς σου

  IId. NON-ISRAELITE:
       τῷ χριστῷ μου Κύρῳ = "to my anointed, Cyrus"
       LXX Isaiah 45:1  — the only LXX case of χριστός applied to a
                          non-Israelite

  IIe. ESCHATOLOGICAL / PROTO-MESSIANIC:
       χριστοῦ ἡγουμένου = "of an anointed leader"
       LXX/Theodotion Daniel 9:25
       ἐξολοθρευθήσεται χριστός = "an anointed one will be cut off"
       Theodotion Daniel 9:26
       [Foundation of Second Temple Jewish messianic chronology]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III.  PROPER NAME / TITLE IN NEW TESTAMENT GREEK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  LSJ III: "ὁ χ., the Messiah (Hebr. מָשִׁיחַ)…
            used as a proper name for Jesus: Ἰησοῦς Χρ., Matt. 1.1"

  a. AS TITLE (with article ὁ):
     ὁ Χριστός = "the Christ / the Messiah"
     Matt 2:4; 16:16; 22:42; Luke 2:26; John 1:20; 7:26–27; Acts 2:36 etc.

  b. AS PROPER NAME (without article):
     Ἰησοῦς Χριστός = personal name "Jesus Christ"
     Matt 1:1; Mark 1:1; Rom 1:1; 1 Cor 1:1; Gal 1:1; Phil 1:1 etc.

  c. BILINGUAL GLOSS:
     Μεσσίαν, ὅ ἐστιν μεθερμηνευόμενον ὁ Χριστός (John 1:41)
     = explicit equation of Aramaic Μεσσίας with Greek ὁ Χριστός

  d. IN PAUL — "ἐν Χριστῷ" formula (locative dative):
     ~164 occurrences of ἐν Χριστῷ / ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ in Pauline corpus
     indicating incorporation into / participation in Christ

  e. EVOLUTION from title to name:
     Classical Greek: χριστός = descriptive adjective (pharmaceutical)
     LXX: ὁ χριστός = institutional title (priestly, royal)
     NT early (Synoptics): ὁ Χριστός = eschatological/messianic title
     NT middle (Paul): Χριστός = proper name (though title retained)
     NT late (John, Rev): both usages in full

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
JOSEPHUS (non-Christian attestation)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Ant. 18.63:  ὁ χριστὸς οὗτος ἦν — "he was the Christ"
               [Testimonium Flavianum; currently debated; possibly partially
                authentic with Christian embellishment]

  Ant. 20.200: τοῦ λεγομένου Χριστοῦ — "of the one called Christ"
               [Widely regarded as authentic; most secure extra-NT attestation]
               λεγόμενος ("so-called / called") signals reporter's neutrality

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RELATED WORDS (same root χρίω)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  χρίω        — verb: "to anoint, smear, rub with oil/fat"
  χρῖσμα      — noun: "anointing, unguent" (1 John 2:20,27; LXX Dan 9:26 LXX)
  χρίσις      — noun: "anointing"
  ἄλειφα      — noun: "ointment" (parallel term)
  κεχρισμένος — perfect passive participle: "having been anointed"
                 (LXX Lev 4:3; used alongside χριστός as title)
  διάχριστος  — adj.: "smeared over, coated" (medical texts: Dioscorides,
                 Galen)
  Μεσσίας     — NT transliteration of Aramaic מְשִׁיחָא (= Greek Χριστός):
                 John 1:41; 4:25

================================================================================
THAYER'S NOTE (NT Greek Lexicon):
  Distinguishes four NT usages:
  1. ὁ Χριστός as Jewish messianic title (the expected deliverer)
  2. Applied to Jesus as fulfillment of OT messianic role
  3. Title becoming proper name (Ἰησοῦς Χριστός)
  4. In plural: possible in early Christian usage (e.g., "false Christs"
     in Matt 24:24 — ψευδόχριστοι)

NOTE ON ψευδόχριστοι (compound):
  Matthew 24:24 / Mark 13:22: ψευδόχριστοι = "false Christs"
  Confirms χριστός was sufficiently technical a title by the time of
  composition that false claimants to the title were a recognized category.
